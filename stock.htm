CVL Rolling Stock Standards


Rolling Stock



ROLLING STOCK

  1. All general rules apply to rolling stock.

  2. Cars shall be weighted as per NMRA Recommended Practice #20.1 :

    Length Weight Length Weight Length Weight Length Weight
    20' - 2.4 OZ 40' - 3.8 OZ 60' - 5.2 OZ 80' - 6.5 OZ
    25' - 2.7 OZ 45' - 4.1 OZ 65' - 5.5 OZ 85' - 6.8 OZ
    30' - 3.1 OZ 50' - 4.5 OZ 70' - 5.8 OZ 90' - 7.2 OZ
    35' - 3.4 OZ 55' - 4.8 OZ 75' - 6.2 OZ - - - - - - - -
    Rolling stock weights must be within 1/2 oz. of spec.

    1. Weights shall be firmly secured to the car.

    2. Unusual cars which cannot be weighted to standard will, upon meeting all other standards, be allowed to operate on the layout per rules II.B.3,4, & 5 following.

    3. Underweight rolling stock shall run at the rear of the train. Overweight rolling stock shall run at the head of the train.

    4. Overweight cars will have one air hose painted white.

    5. Underweight cars will have one air hose painted orange.

  3. Unit trains.

    1. Unit trains, for the purpose of this standard, shall be trains intended to be a permanent group. This does not include general revenue freight cars.

    2. If a unit train cannot be made to conform to standards, it can still be run on the main line, so long as it can operate consistently on the main line, and has Kadee couplers at the head and tail ends which conform to standards.

  4. Trucks must rotate freely, must be able to negotiate a #4 switch without binding or derailment, and must have a slight lateral rock.

  5. Rolling stock must be able to roll unassisted down a 3% grade.

  6. The owner's color code markings shall be on the frame or undercarriage of all rolling stock.
